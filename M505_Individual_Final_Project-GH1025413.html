PART A¶

PROJECT OVERVIEW: SpaceMine is a Space mining company which aims to extract geologically and economically minerals from asteroids in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupyter.¶

Key Idea: When struck by cosmic waves chemical elements in soils and rocks emit uniquely identifiable signatures in the form of gamma rays. This data can help SpaceMine to identify potential mines in the asteroid belt. However, unlike optical and x-rays, gamma rays can not be captured by mirrors. They have the highest frequency and are statistically reconstructed to simulate registration of high energy gamma particles in a ground-based atmospheric Cherenkov gamma telescope using the imaging technique.¶

Understanding Business Problem:¶

Problem Statement: But, the simulated data has one problem. It consists of primary gamma (Signal) generated data as well as the data from background cosmic waves (noise) by hadron collisions. Separating this data is not humanely possible due to two reasons:¶

1- The data is not an image but a collection of focal attributes in the form of numbers.¶

2- The data is huge and processing and predicting individual records is not possible.¶

Task: Thus, we need to segregate the two classes i.e., gamma(signal) and hadron(noise) by training a machine learning classification model so that SpaceMine can accurately detect the potential space mining regions and can capitalize on the AI driven business insights.¶

Moreover, this pioneering work would unleash a new era of socio-economic boom, which would bring the prices of precious minerals down and promote global prosperity alongwith environment sustainability.¶

The relevant data is collected from UCI Machine Learning Repository¶

Source of data:¶

UCI Machine Learning Repository. https://doi.org/10.24432/C52C8B.

Formulating the problem as a machine learning task¶

As a newly appointed data scientist at SpaceMine I shall make an end-to-end data pipeline starting with evaluating the dataset , wrangling and transforming it and making it ready to train it in multiple machine learning models. I shall train multiple classificatory models to distinguish the signal (gamma) from the noise(hadron) and evaluate it on different model hyper-parameters. I would parallelly evaluate the models with their classification reports to bring out the best possible predictor. Finally, I would provide a business solution highlighting various strengths and limitations of my pipeline and give recommendations on the business solution. This would enable SpaceMine to get a competitive edge with AI driven decision making it identify potential mining areas in the Asteroid Belt.¶

def outlier(data):
    anomaly = []
    sd = statistics.stdev(data)
    mean = statistics.mean(data)
    cutoff = sd*3
    for item in data:
        if item > mean+cutoff or item < mean-cutoff:
            anomaly.append(item)
    return anomaly

Inference¶

We are not removing the outliers since we are training a classification model and we want our model to be trained on extreme cases as well, to make it more robust and prevent over-fitting. We want our model to have high variance (complex) and at the same time low bias, so that it understands the complex nature of our data samples but also gives less error.¶

Which evaluation metrics fit this dataset?¶

Explaining the dataset dependent and independent features (columns)¶

INDEPENDENT VARIABLES (10)¶

1. FL: (FOCAL LENGTH): Ellipse major axis in mm¶

2. FW: (FOCAL WIDTH): Ellipse minor axis in mm¶

3. FS: (SIZE): Log (Sum of all pixel values)¶

4. FC: Sum of two highest pixels / FS¶

5. FC1: Highest pixel / FS¶

6. FA: Distance from highest pixel to center, projected onto major axis in mm¶

7. FM3L: 3rd root of 3rdment along major axis in mm¶

8. FM3T: 3rd root of 3rd moment of minor axis in mm¶

9. FAlpha: Angle of major axis with vector to origin in degrees¶

10. FD: Distance from origin to center of ellipse in mm¶

DEPENDENT TARGET VARIABLE:¶

CLASS: g,h for gamma (SIGNAL) and hadron (NOISE)¶

This is a classification problem and various classification metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, Area under ROC curve will be evaluated. However, accuracy is not meaningful here because classifying a hadron(noise) as gamma(signal) will waste SpaceMine's time, money and resources in pursuing a futile exploration due to our misclassification. Hence, we need to minimize False Positives. We give priority to PRECISION when the cost of False Positive is high (in this case) and we prioritize RECALL when the cost of False Negative is high (eg. Cancer Prediction, Spam, etc). The cost of false negative is not very high, since even if we miss some potential areas it will not cost SpaceMine significant costs because there are many potential sites. Thus, PRECISION is vital here. Also AUC-ROC will be given importance during model evaluation step to evaluate the overall performance of the model.This is significant as AUC-ROC is scale-invariant and is a key measure when class is im-balanced and when the cost of False Positive and False Negative are different as in this case.

KEY METRIC IN OUR FOCUS: PRECISION AND AUC-ROC¶

PART F¶

A final discussion on the overall pipeline. Strengths, Limitations and Recommendations. What are the most informative features of your model? Is your model explainable?¶

The overall pipeline is based on the classification problem of classifying the target variable into Gamma (signal) or Hadron (noise). It covers the major models and evaluation criterias.¶

Strengths :¶

1- Data has been pre-processed and can be validated.¶

2- Covers most of the classification models and evaluation metrics.¶

3- The solution picks the right evalution metric i.e. precision and auc-roc¶

4- The problem statement and task at hand is well defined¶

5- XGBoost uses parallel and distributed computing. Thus, it is fast and can be integrated dynamically during model deployment using Flask and Heroku.¶

Limitations:¶

1- The proposed solution requires more hyperparameter tuning which was deterred by less computational power. Eg GridSearch was taking a lot of time¶

2- The use of neural networks can also be done. However, for this dataset it would not be the right trade-off.¶

3- More data can be helpful to decrease over-fitting and help in increasing the predictive power of the model.¶

4- The final model uses XGBoost which is less interpretable as compared to other models like logistic rregression and Random Forest. It is considered a black box model.¶

5- Lasso and Ridge regularization can also be used with XGBoost to reduce overfitting and improve generalization performance.¶

Recommendations:¶

1- A higher ROC-AUC metric shows that the model is better in distinguishing between the two classes. Hence, SpaceMine can trust the machine learning model.¶

2- It can create a full-fledged report from these results to improve investor confidence.¶

3- A higher Recall can help SpaceMine to discover other potential sites by decreasing the False Negatives and a higher Precision can help SpaceMine to not undertake futile explorations¶

4- SpaceMine should target on creating a full data warehouse so that more features can be measured which can help in better predictions. However, SpaceMine should focus more on the state of the art technology to retrieve precious minerals once it gets a nod of the potential site from the model.¶

5- The prediciton once made should be cross-validated with other models and hyper-parameters.¶

6- As data will grow SpaceMine should switch to other models as XGBoost is memory intensive in nature due to the size of its hyparameter space.¶
